InsiderScreener vs InsidersAlpha

Both platforms track MAR Article 19 European insider disclosures. InsiderScreener is an established tool with broad global reach. InsidersAlpha focuses on European depth, active buyback tracking, and conviction signals. This page compares them objectively, feature by feature.

Quick summary

InsiderScreener

Broad global coverage, strong analyst tools

16 markets including US, Canada, Australia, and India alongside 12 European markets. Good insider performance rankings with pre-planned trade filtering. Clean REST API. Best suited to investors who track both US and European markets from one tool.

InsidersAlpha

European depth, buybacks, and conviction scoring

13 markets (12 EU + South Korea), with Norway as a key European market not covered by InsiderScreener. Unique active buyback tracking across 1,750+ programs, a 4-layer signal system, and algorithmic conviction scoring. Very generous free tier.

What InsiderScreener does well

InsiderScreener is an established platform with a good reputation in the European insider data space. These are its genuine strengths:

🌍

Broadest global market reach

16 markets including US, Canada, Australia, and India alongside 12 European markets. The only option here if you need a single tool that covers both US and EU insider data.

🏆

Insider performance rankings

Top Insiders feature ranks insiders by average return and success rate with pre-planned trade filtering. Useful for identifying historically accurate buyers before following their next trade.

🔍

Cluster buy screener

Cluster buying detection is confirmed. Their screener lets you filter for multi-insider buy events across their full market universe.

🔌

REST API for developers

A documented REST API is available on paid plans (Starter and Pro tiers). Good option for developers building their own tools on top of the data.

What InsidersAlpha adds

1. Active buyback program tracking — 1,750+ programs

Tracks authorised share buyback programs across Norway, UK, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, and Belgium. Each program shows progress bars, completion percentage, end date, and remaining budget. No other free platform offers this. InsiderScreener does not appear to track buyback programs.

2. 4-layer signal system

Cluster buying (2+ insiders, 14-day window) · Repetitive buying (same insider, multiple purchases) · Price dip (10–60% drawdown before purchase) · Pre-earnings (30–45 days before blackout period). These stack: a cluster buy during a price dip is surfaced with both badges simultaneously.

3. Norway coverage (Oslo Bors)

InsidersAlpha scrapes Finanstilsynet daily for Norwegian insider disclosures. InsiderScreener's 16-market coverage does not include Norway, making this a meaningful gap for European investors.

4. South Korea via DART

Korean insider disclosures from DART alongside European coverage. Useful for investors with exposure to Korean-listed holdings.

5. Conviction scoring (V3)

Each buy transaction receives an algorithmic conviction score based on cluster activity, repetition pattern, purchase size, and insider seniority. Surfaces the highest-signal trades from daily filing volume without manual filtering.

6. Generous free tier

First 50 transactions per market visible without an account. Signal badges, top 5 insiders, and buyback dashboard accessible on free tier. No credit card required to evaluate.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between InsiderScreener and InsidersAlpha?

Both track European MAR Art.19 insider disclosures. InsidersAlpha additionally offers active buyback program tracking across 6 markets (1,750+ programs), a 4-layer conviction signal system, South Korea coverage via DART, and insider performance tracking at 30, 90, and 180 days. InsiderScreener has broader global reach (US, Canada, Australia, India) and confirmed pre-planned trade filtering.

Does InsiderScreener track share buybacks?

InsiderScreener does not appear to offer share buyback program tracking. InsidersAlpha tracks 1,750+ active buyback programs across Norway, UK, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, and Belgium with progress bars and completion percentages. This is a category where the two platforms differ meaningfully.

Which platform has better Nordic coverage?

InsidersAlpha covers all 4 Nordic markets — Sweden, Denmark, Norway, and Finland. InsiderScreener covers Sweden, Denmark, and Finland but does not include Norway as of 2026. For investors focused on Oslo Bors, InsidersAlpha is the relevant choice.

Does InsidersAlpha cover more markets than InsiderScreener?

InsiderScreener covers more markets in total (16 including US, Australia, India, and Canada). InsidersAlpha focuses on 13 markets (12 European + South Korea). Norway and South Korea are markets InsidersAlpha covers that InsiderScreener does not; US, Canada, Australia, and India are markets InsiderScreener covers that InsidersAlpha does not.

Which platform is better for a developer or quant?

Both offer REST APIs on paid plans. InsiderScreener's API starts at €25/mo. InsidersAlpha's API is available on the Elite plan at €14.99/mo (annual). InsiderScreener has a longer track record with developer integrations. InsidersAlpha's API is newer but covers the buyback and signal data layers not available from InsiderScreener.
